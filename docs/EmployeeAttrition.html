Employee Attrition Analysis

Overview: This project focuses on understanding employee attrition, why do employees leave their companies? is it because of the work environment, salary, or other factors? The goal is to analyze the data and provide insights that can help organizations improve retention strategies.

In this project, I have used a dataset of employees from a company with over 1400 employees, analyzing various factors such as job satisfaction, salary, and work-life balance to answer:

  • What is the attrition rate for the company?
  • What are the main factors contributing to attrition?
  • Do employees that leave have any common factors between them?
  • Do demographics of employees impact the attrition rate?

And more questions that can be answered by analyzing the data. The analysis includes visualizations and statistical methods to draw conclusions and provide actionable insights for the company to improve employee retention.

Tools used: PowerBI only have been used, as the dataset was clean and prepared for analysis

Attrition & Satisfaction Rates

First stage of the analysis, it aims to understand the attrition, satsisfaction & environment satisfaction rates for the employees in the company, and how do these 3 correlate to each. the insights uncovered were:

  • Attrition rate is 16.1% which is a bit high, as the attrition rate for most companies is around 13%
  • Both satisfaction rate and enviroment satisfaction rate are around 68%, this is higher than average
  • Attrition rate has a negative correlation with environment satisfaction rate & satisfaction rate for company

Demographics

Second stage of the analysis, it aims to understand the demographics of the employees in the company, and how do these demographics impact the attrition rate. The insights uncovered:

  • Attrition rate for female employees is 22% and 17% for males, this means female employees are more likely to leave
  • Attrition rate is extremely high for employees aged 18-22, very high for 22-30, at its lowest for employees aged 30-50, moderately high for employees above 50
  • Employees with HR Education field are most likely to leave
  • Employees that are unsatisfied in their relationships or single are likely to leave

Travel & Organization

Third stage, it uncovers the impact of travel and organization on attrition rates. Key insights:

  • Almost 40% of sales representatives have left the company which is quiet alot. Comming after them laboratory technichans and HRs with both roles having almost 24% Attrition rate
  • Sales department has the highest attrition rate, by 38.5%
  • Over 50% of employees lost were frequent travellers

Time spent in company

Stage 4, it focuses on the time employees spend in the company and its correlation with attrition rates. Key insights:

  • Employees who either joined the company recently [within 1-3 years] and employees who stayed for too long [22+ years] are most likely to leave
  • Employees who stayed too long in their roles or with their managers are most likely to leave

Compensation Rates

One of the most important, if not the most imporant factors in employee retention is compensation. This stage of the analysis focuses on understanding how compensation rates impact attrition:

  • Average salary for employees in company is 6500$ which is higher than average in USA [6000$]
  • Attrition rate decreases as salary increases with a slightly weak positive correlation
  • Attrition rate decreases slightly as percent salary hike increases
  • Employees with high daily rates are less likely to leave the company

Other metrics

Final stage of the analysis, it focuses on other metrics that impact attrition rates. Key insights:

  • Employees who have worked in multipe companies before [5-9] are most likely to leave, while employees who worked with 0-4 companies are less likely to leave
  • Employees who were given 2 or 3 trainings were most likely to leave too
  • Most employees who left were living near the company

What should we do to decrease the attrition rate?

Based on the analysis, here are some recommendations to decrease the attrition rate:

  • Improve work-life balance and job satisfaction by providing better work environment and support
  • Increase salary and compensation rates to match industry standards
  • Focus on employee development and training to improve skills and job satisfaction
  • Implement flexible work arrangements to accommodate employees' needs
